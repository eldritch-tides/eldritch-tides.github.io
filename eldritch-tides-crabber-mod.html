The Crabber's Method
Drop, Wait, Haul

Unlike the active fishing minigame, crabbing is entirely passive. Set your pots and go about your voyage. The sea does the work.

🪤
Drop
Press G anywhere on open water. One pot leaves your hold and sinks to the bottom. You get a hint about the spot's quality.
⏳
Wait
Pots fill over 45–90 seconds. Each holds up to 3 catches. Fill speed is boosted by nearby kelp, wrecks, and fishing buoys.
✅
Return
Sail back within 220 units of your pot. A glow ring and G prompt appear. Full pots glow gold and show a FULL label.
📦
Haul
Press G to haul. Catches go to cargo, pot returns to your hold. Watch for the 8% crab incident on the way up.

Reading the Water
Hot Spots

Not all water is equal. Drop your pots near points of interest and your catch rate climbs significantly. The sea rewards those who know where to look.

🌿
Kelp Beds
Dense kelp forests shelter crabs and shrimp in their roots. The most productive coastal spots on the map. Approach slowly — the kelp slows you too.
1.6× fill speed
⚓
Shipwrecks
Debris fields attract bottom feeders and opportunistic crabs. The wreck structure creates shelter. Rich pickings in the deep decay.
1.4× fill speed
🔴
Fishing Buoys
Other fisherfolk know the good spots. Buoys mark productive grounds used across generations. The water here holds memory.
1.3× fill speed

Occupational Hazard
The Crab Incident
🦀
They Get Loose Sometimes
Every pot haul carries an 8% chance of a crab incident — the pot comes up tangled, a large specimen breaks free, and before you can do anything sensible about it there are claws involved. The hull takes 3–10 points of damage. The crabs disappear overboard. You write it off as a maritime reality and haul the next pot.
8% chance per haul · 3–10 hull damage · 1 splash effect
Keep a repair kit on board if you're running deep water pots heavily. The math catches up with you eventually.
The Simple Instructions
How It Works

One key. Multiple pots. A good route and patience.

Deploy Pots

Press G on open water to drop a pot from your hold. You start with 3. The log tells you if the spot is notable — kelp, wreck, buoy, or deep.

Plan a Route

You have up to 5 pots deployed at once (8 with the Crabber's Kit). Drop them in a circuit — fish, trade, or raid between hauls while your pots fill.

Watch the Glow

Pots show a fill arc around the float buoy as they fill. Green pips appear for each catch. When full, the buoy turns gold and shows FULL.

Haul When Ready

Within 220 units, press G again to haul. Catches go straight to cargo. The pot returns to your hold. Don't wait too long — pots despawn after 5 minutes.

Restock at Merchants

Merchant ships sell Crab Pots ×3 for 60 ⊕ and the Crabber's Kit for 280 ⊕ — a one-time upgrade expanding capacity to 8 simultaneous pots.

Go Deep

Sail at least 350 units from any island and drop a pot. The deep table unlocks — King Crab, Dungeness, Silver Eel, and the prize catches: Sailfish and Sea Turtle.

Get to Work
Enable the Mod

One line. Three pots. Ready to go.

Edit mods/index.json:

["crabber-life"]

Or combine with the Pirate Life mod — they play together without conflict:

["pirate-life", "crabber-life"]
// Drop pots. Raid a charter ship. Haul while evading the Navy.
// The crabs don't care about your wanted level.

Then run ./serve.sh and open http://localhost:8080. You start with 3 crab pots.
