The Pirate's Handbook
How It Works

Five actions. One escalating threat. Endless greed.

Spot a Charter Ship

Merchant vessels appear on the open sea. Sail close — within 300 units — until the hail prompt appears in the log. They won't run. Not yet.

Press Q to Intercept

Near a merchant, Q opens the intercept panel instead of the normal shop. Choose TRADE for the normal merchant experience, or RAID ☠ to take everything.

Collect the Plunder

Gold is added instantly. The merchant ship sinks. Your wanted level rises by one star. The Navy has been notified. You have about 50 seconds before they arrive.

Fight or Flee

Use C to fire cannons at pursuing Navy ships. Sinking one gives bounty gold and drops your wanted level. Or just run — the Navy can't pursue indefinitely.

Clear Your Bounty

Dock at any port and press B to bribe the harbourmaster. Or find Forged Papers in merchant stock. Or wait — at ★★ and below, time clears your name.

Survive ★★★★★

Four Navy ships, 8-second spawns, 1.4s fire rate. Your hull will not last long. At this point you are either committed or you're debris. There is no middle position.

The Captain's Calculus
Risk vs. Reward

Every star you earn raises both what you take and what hunts you. The math never quite works out in your favour. That's the point.

Enable the Mod

One line. That's it.

Edit mods/index.json in your project folder:

["pirate-life"]

Or run alongside the Crabber Life mod — they're fully compatible:

["pirate-life", "crabber-life"]
// Drop crab pots. Raid charter ships while you wait.
// The crabs don't care about your wanted level.

Then run ./serve.sh (or serve.bat on Windows) and open http://localhost:8080.
